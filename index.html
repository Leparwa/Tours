Why put Baringo In Your Touring Destination List?

Resting in the Maginificient Great Rift Valley, Baringo is Characterised by hautingly beautiful natural Lake, Spa, Landscapes of forested hills with an an extraordinary abudance of wildlife. The county's diverse range of traditional African cultures is influenced by over 7 unique ethnic groups from Ilchamus Tugen, Pokot, Turkana, Nubians, Endorois and Luo communities. Apart from diverse cultures from the above mentioned communities, A unique Lake Breeze of Lake Baringo, Hotsprings In Lake Bogoria, Different types of birds living along the Shores of Lake Baringo and Natural Spa resort provides an exccellent to visitors. Many Local and International Tourist flock every to experience a trully unique African adventure in one of the most Pristine safari Destination.

About LakeBARINGO

History

The name "Baringo" is derived from Ilchamus's community phrase "Mparingo" which translate to Lake. Its basin Extends a surface Area of about 108 square Kilometers, and an elavation of 970 metres (3,180 ft)

Location

It is one of the Northern Kenya's Rift Valley Fresh Water Lakes, approximately 230Km (4hour drive) from Capital city of kenya-Nairobi. It is Located in the Administrative County of Baringo at an altitude of 1,000km above the sea Level.

Unique Features

It has several small Islands. Ol Kokwe is one of the largest Island. an extinct Volcano which is located at the north of Lake, has several Hot springs and fumaroles, some of which have precipated sulfur deposits.

Bio Life

Since it is a fresh water Lake, The Lake host a variety of Fish Species. Tilapia being the predominant fish in the Lake. the Lake is also inhabited by Crocodiles, hippopotamuses and Various Bird Species along the shores of the lake.

Communitis Around The Lake

the Ilchamus and Tugen are two communities that lives around The Lake. The Ichamus community belong the sub-tribe of Maa Community, while Tugen belong to the Sub-tribe of Kalenjin Community.

Economic Activities

the Ilchamus community being one of sub tribe of highland nilotes of Kenya, their main Economic Activity is livestock keeping. While their counterpart, The Tugen Community majorly depend on Farming and Bee Keeping.
